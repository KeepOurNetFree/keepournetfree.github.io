Dear { Congressman },

A bill has recently passed in the Senate that puts the privacy of all citizens who use the Internet at risk. This deadly bill is referred to as S.J.Res.34, and it allows any Internet Service Provider (ISP) to distribute and sell confidential information of its users. Information trading is not a new practice, but it has previously only been performed by companies like Google and Facebook. These companies are different than an ISP, because you had to opt-in to them, but providing Internet is a near-monopoly, and someone who lives in modern times cannot avoid using the Internet. If this bill passes, the information of every American citizen, including you, would potentially be available to everyone. This information includes, but is not limited to:

• Confidential Medical Information. If you’ve ever searched the Internet for a specific symptom, then someone can learn that and infer whatever you are suffering from.

• Browsing Habits. Whether you use the Internet for research, work, social media, or pornography, the entire world can pay to know. This is not simply what sites you visit, but what you are attempting to find. This can clue political opponents in to what you are researching for an upcoming debate over a bill or even let an opponent run a smear campaign over some embarrassing website you went to one time. This can also allow for illegal discrimination from an employer; for example, if you regularly visit websites with content an employer disagrees with, the hiring manager can look at your history and decide to not hire you.

As a concerned member of the community that you represent, I urge you to vote no on S.J.Res.34.

Sincerely,
