Background

On March 7, 2017, Senate Joint Resolution 34 (S.J. Res 34) was introduced to the US Senate by 23 Senators. On March 23, 2017, S.J. Res 34 passed the US senate with a 50-48 vote.

This was the first step to allow Internet service providers (ISPs) to sell private browsing information and app data usage to third parties. What's worse, they can manipulate your Internet experience with ad injections and search redirections. This is a massive violation of privacy and should be a major concern to all citizens.

The resolution is not yet law as the resolution still requires a majority vote in the House of Respresentatives. The House version of this resolution (H.J. Res 86) is scheduled to be voted on the week of March 27, 2017.

The time to take action is now: Share this website with your friends, family, and fellow Internet users.
